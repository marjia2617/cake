Opera Cake Recipe

Opera Cake is a classic French dessert known for its elegant layers and rich flavors, combining almond sponge cake (known as Joconde), coffee buttercream, and chocolate ganache. Here’s a detailed recipe for making this sophisticated dessert:

Classic Opera Cake Recipe

Ingredients

For the Joconde (Almond Sponge Cake):

  • 1 cup (100 g) almond flour
  • 1 cup (120 g) powdered sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 large egg white
  • 2 tablespoons (30 g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup (65 g) all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of salt

For the Coffee Syrup:

  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) water
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons instant coffee or espresso powder
  • 1 tablespoon coffee liqueur (optional)

For the Coffee Buttercream:

  • 1 cup (225 g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 2 tablespoons instant coffee or espresso powder dissolved in 1 tablespoon hot water

For the Chocolate Ganache:

  • 6 ounces (170 g) dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) heavy cream

For the Glaze:

  • 4 ounces (115 g) dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) heavy cream

Instructions

  • Prepare the Joconde:
    • Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Grease and line a half-sheet pan (18x13 inches) with parchment paper.
    • In a mixing bowl, combine almond flour, powdered sugar, and whole eggs. Whisk until smooth.
    • In another bowl, whip egg whites with a pinch of salt until soft peaks form. Gradually add granulated sugar and continue whipping until stiff peaks form.
    • Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the almond mixture.
    • Sift the all-purpose flour over the mixture and fold in gently. Finally, fold in the melted butter.
    • Pour the batter onto the prepared sheet pan, spreading it evenly. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
    • Let it cool completely in the pan, then invert onto a clean surface and remove the parchment paper.

  • Make the Coffee Syrup:
    • In a small saucepan, combine water and sugar. Bring to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
    • Remove from heat and stir in the coffee and coffee liqueur (if using). Let it cool.

  • Prepare the Coffee Buttercream:
    • In a heatproof bowl, combine sugar and egg whites. Set over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and whisk until the mixture reaches 160°F (70°C).
    • Remove from heat and whip the mixture until it cools and forms stiff peaks.
    • Gradually add softened butter, continuing to whip until smooth. Mix in the dissolved coffee.

  • Make the Chocolate Ganache:
    • Place chopped chocolate in a bowl. Heat cream until just boiling, then pour over the chocolate. Let it sit for a minute, then stir until smooth. Let it cool slightly.

  • Assemble the Opera Cake:
    • Cut the Joconde into three equal rectangles (9x6 inches).
    • Place one layer of Joconde on a serving platter. Brush generously with coffee syrup.
    • Spread half of the coffee buttercream over the layer, followed by a layer of chocolate ganache.
    • Add a second layer of Joconde, brush with coffee syrup, and repeat the buttercream and ganache layers.
    • Add the final layer of Joconde, brush with syrup, and refrigerate for about 30 minutes to set.

  • Prepare the Glaze:
    • Heat cream until just boiling. Pour over the chopped chocolate and let it sit for a minute. Stir until smooth.
    • Pour the glaze over the top of the chilled cake, letting it drip down the sides. Smooth with a spatula if needed.

  • Final Touches:
    • Refrigerate the cake for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.
    • Before serving, you can decorate the top with chocolate decorations or a thin layer of gold leaf for a luxurious touch.
    • Slice into rectangles for serving.
