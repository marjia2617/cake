History of Torta Setteveli

Torta Setteveli, or the "Seven Veils Cake," is a luxurious multi-layered dessert originating from Sicily, Italy. It was created as a modern masterpiece in the world of patisserie, combining chocolate and hazelnut flavors with artistic precision.
The name "Setteveli" means "seven veils," referring to the seven distinct layers of textures and flavors in the cake. While its exact origins are debated, it is often attributed to the World Pastry Cup in Lyon, France, in 1997, where Italian pastry chefs brought fame to this intricate dessert. It has since become a symbol of indulgence and craftsmanship in Italian baking.

Traditional Recipe for Torta Setteveli

Ingredients

For the Layers:

  1. Hazelnut Dacquoise (Almond Sponge):
    • 100 g hazelnut flour
    • 100 g powdered sugar
    • 4 egg whites
    • 40 g granulated sugar
  2. Chocolate Mousse:
    • 200 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
    • 300 ml heavy cream
    • 3 egg yolks
    • 50 g granulated sugar
    • 5 g gelatin, soaked
  3. Hazelnut Bavarois:
    • 200 ml milk
    • 3 egg yolks
    • 50 g sugar
    • 5 g gelatin, soaked
    • 100 g hazelnut paste
    • 200 ml whipped cream
  4. Crispy Praline Layer:
    • 100 g praline paste
    • 50 g dark chocolate, melted
    • 50 g feuilletine (crispy wafer flakes)
  5. Chocolate Glaze:
    • 150 g dark chocolate
    • 100 ml heavy cream
    • 20 g butter

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Hazelnut Dacquoise:
    • Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    • Whisk egg whites to soft peaks, gradually adding granulated sugar until stiff peaks form.
    • Fold in hazelnut flour and powdered sugar. Spread the mixture onto the baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes. Cool completely and cut into rounds.
  2. Make the Chocolate Mousse:
    • Melt the chocolate in a double boiler and let it cool slightly.
    • Whip the cream to soft peaks and set aside.
    • Beat egg yolks with sugar until pale, then add the soaked gelatin and melted chocolate. Fold in whipped cream gently.
  3. Prepare the Hazelnut Bavarois:
    • Heat milk in a saucepan. Whisk egg yolks and sugar, then slowly pour in hot milk, stirring constantly.
    • Cook over low heat until thickened. Add gelatin, hazelnut paste, and fold in whipped cream.
  4. Create the Praline Layer:
    • Mix praline paste, melted chocolate, and feuilletine. Spread a thin layer over one of the dacquoise rounds and refrigerate until set.
  5. Assemble the Cake:
    • In a cake ring, layer dacquoise, chocolate mousse, hazelnut bavarois, and repeat. Ensure the praline layer is near the bottom for structural integrity. Chill between layers for clean separation.
  6. Apply the Chocolate Glaze:
    • Heat cream and pour over chopped chocolate. Stir until smooth and glossy. Add butter for shine. Pour over the chilled cake and allow it to set.
  7. Chill and Serve:
    • Refrigerate the cake for at least 6 hours or overnight. Decorate with chocolate curls or hazelnut praline before serving.

Tips for Making Torta Setteveli

  1. Plan Ahead:
    • This is a time-intensive recipe. Prepare some components, like the praline paste or dacquoise, in advance.
  2. Use Quality Ingredients:
    • Premium dark chocolate and hazelnut paste elevate the flavor significantly.
  3. Work in Layers:
    • Chill each layer before adding the next to ensure clean, defined layers.
  4. Feuilletine Substitute:
    • If you can't find feuilletine, use crushed cornflakes or wafer cookies for the crispy praline layer.
  5. Gelatin Technique:
    • Dissolve gelatin properly in warm liquid to avoid lumps.
  6. Perfect Glaze:
    • Pour the glaze over the cake on a wire rack for an even finish, catching excess glaze below.

Best Time to Bake Torta Setteveli

Torta Setteveli is ideal for special occasions and festive events due to its intricate design and luxurious taste. It is particularly popular during:

  • Christmas and New Year: Its rich chocolate and hazelnut flavors suit winter celebrations.
  • Weddings or Anniversaries: Its elegance makes it a showstopper for formal events.
  • Birthdays or Milestones: A true masterpiece for memorable moments.

This cake is best served chilled, making it suitable year-round, especially in cooler months when rich desserts are most appreciated.
