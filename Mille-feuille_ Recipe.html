Mille-Feuille (also known as Napoleon) is a classic French pastry made up of layers of crispy puff pastry and smooth pastry cream, often topped with a beautiful glaze. It’s a delicate dessert with a perfect balance of textures. Here's a recipe with some tips to ensure success.

Mille-Feuille Recipe

Ingredients

For the Puff Pastry:

  • 1 pound (450 g) puff pastry (store-bought or homemade)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

For the Pastry Cream:

  • 2 cups (500 ml) whole milk
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup (30 g) cornstarch
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract (or 1 vanilla bean)
  • 2 tablespoons (30 g) unsalted butter

For the Icing:

  • 1 cup (125 g) powdered sugar
  • 2–3 tablespoons water
  • 1/4 cup (30 g) dark chocolate (for the chocolate lines)

Instructions


Prepare the Puff Pastry

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry to about 1/8 inch (3 mm) thick and trim the edges to create a neat rectangle. Transfer the pastry to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Prick the entire surface of the puff pastry with a fork to prevent it from puffing up too much during baking.
  4. Place another sheet of parchment paper on top of the puff pastry and weigh it down with another baking sheet or a heavy pan (to ensure it stays flat).
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown. Remove the top baking sheet and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes to let the pastry crisp up.
  6. Once baked, let the pastry cool, and then cut it into three equal rectangles.

Make the Pastry Cream

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat the milk and vanilla over medium heat until just boiling. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch until pale and smooth.
  3. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture while whisking continuously to temper the eggs.
  4. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan and cook over medium heat, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens (about 3-4 minutes).
  5. Remove from heat, whisk in the butter, and transfer the pastry cream to a bowl. Cover with plastic wrap touching the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Let it cool completely in the fridge.

Assemble the Mille-Feuille

  1. Place one layer of baked puff pastry on a serving plate.
  2. Spread half of the pastry cream evenly over the pastry.
  3. Place the second layer of puff pastry on top and spread the remaining pastry cream.
  4. Top with the third layer of puff pastry.

Prepare the Icing

  1. In a small bowl, mix the powdered sugar with enough water to create a smooth, spreadable glaze.
  2. Melt the dark chocolate and transfer it to a piping bag (or a small plastic bag with the tip cut off).
  3. Spread the white icing over the top layer of the puff pastry.
  4. Pipe thin parallel lines of chocolate across the icing, then use a toothpick to drag through the chocolate lines in alternating directions to create a feathered pattern.

Chill and Serve

  1. Refrigerate the assembled Mille-Feuille for at least 1 hour to allow the pastry cream to set and the flavors to meld.
  2. When ready to serve, use a sharp knife to carefully slice into portions.
