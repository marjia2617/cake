History of Amor Polenta

Amor Polenta, also known as Dolce Varese, is a traditional cake from Varese, a town in the Lombardy region of northern Italy. The name “Amor Polenta” refers to the cake’s key ingredient: polenta flour (cornmeal), which gives it a distinctive texture and rustic charm. It is also a symbol of Varese's culinary identity.
The cake was created in the late 19th or early 20th century as an homage to the simplicity of rural Italian cuisine. Its recipe combines polenta with almonds, butter, and eggs, creating a moist, nutty cake with a golden hue. Traditionally baked in a special ridged loaf pan, Amor Polenta has become a classic Italian dessert, appreciated for its subtle sweetness and versatility.

Amor Polenta Recipe

Ingredients

  • Dry Ingredients:
    • 1/2 cup (80 g) cornmeal or polenta flour (medium-grain works best)
    • 1/2 cup (80 g) almond flour (finely ground almonds)
    • 1/2 cup (80 g) all-purpose flour
    • 1 tsp baking powder
    • Pinch of salt
  • Wet Ingredients:
    • 1/2 cup (120 g) unsalted butter, softened
    • 2/3 cup (130 g) granulated sugar
    • 3 large eggs
    • 2 tbsp milk
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Optional:
    • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pan:
    • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a ridged loaf pan or a standard loaf pan.
  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients:
    • In a bowl, combine the cornmeal, almond flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Cream the Butter and Sugar:
    • In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar together until light and fluffy.
  4. Incorporate the Eggs:
    • Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the milk, vanilla extract, and lemon zest.
  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients:
    • Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture until fully combined, being careful not to overmix.
  6. Bake the Cake:
    • Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool and Serve:
    • Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Tips for Making Amor Polenta

  1. Use Medium-Grain Polenta:
    • Avoid coarse polenta, which can make the cake too gritty. A finer grind creates the perfect texture.
  2. Don't Skip the Almond Flour:
    • Almond flour adds richness and a nutty flavor. For a stronger almond taste, you can add a few drops of almond extract.
  3. Use a Ridged Loaf Pan:
    • If possible, use the traditional pan for an authentic look. If unavailable, a regular loaf pan works fine.
  4. Balance the Moisture:
    • Ensure the butter is fully softened, and do not overbake to prevent dryness.
  5. Customize Flavors:
    • Add a splash of orange liqueur or substitute the lemon zest with orange zest for a variation.
  6. Store Properly:
    • Amor Polenta keeps well for several days if wrapped tightly or stored in an airtight container.

Best Season and Occasion to Bake Amor Polenta

Amor Polenta is a year-round dessert that shines in its simplicity. However, it pairs particularly well with spring and summer, thanks to its light, citrusy flavor. It’s perfect for a tea party, brunch, or as an elegant afternoon snack.
For occasions, it’s a versatile cake that works equally well for casual gatherings or more formal celebrations, particularly when paired with espresso, a dollop of whipped cream, or fresh fruit. It’s also a thoughtful gift due to its long shelf life and compact size.
