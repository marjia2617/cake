History of Ricotta and Pear Cake

Ricotta and Pear Cake, or Torta di Ricotta e Pere, is a delightful Italian dessert believed to have originated in Campania, particularly in the Amalfi Coast region. This cake showcases two essential ingredients of Italian cuisine: ricotta cheese, known for its creamy, light texture, and pears, a fruit often associated with Italian desserts for their sweetness and juiciness.
The cake was popularized by Salvatore De Riso, a renowned pastry chef from the Amalfi Coast, who refined the recipe into a modern classic. It features a harmonious balance of light ricotta cream, fragrant pears, and soft hazelnut sponge cake, making it both elegant and approachable. This dessert is a celebration of simplicity, allowing the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine.

Classic Ricotta and Pear Cake Recipe

Ingredients

  • For the Hazelnut Sponge Layers:
    1. 1 1/4 cups (150 g) hazelnut flour (or ground hazelnuts)
    2. 1/2 cup (60 g) all-purpose flour
    3. 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
    4. 4 large eggs (separated)
    5. 1/4 cup (60 g) unsalted butter, melted
    6. Pinch of salt
  • For the Ricotta Cream:
    1. 1 1/4 cups (300 g) fresh ricotta cheese (preferably sheep’s milk ricotta)
    2. 1/2 cup (100 g) powdered sugar
    3. 1 cup (250 ml) heavy cream, whipped
    4. 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • For the Pear Filling:
    1. 2 medium pears (ripe but firm), peeled and diced
    2. 2 tbsp sugar
    3. 1 tbsp lemon juice
    4. 1 tbsp pear liqueur or water (optional)
  • For Garnish:
    1. Powdered sugar
    2. Toasted hazelnuts or chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Hazelnut Sponge:
    • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line two 9-inch (23 cm) round cake pans.
    • In a mixing bowl, beat the egg yolks and sugar until pale and fluffy. Gradually add the melted butter.
    • In a separate bowl, whisk the egg whites with a pinch of salt until stiff peaks form.
    • Gently fold the hazelnut flour, all-purpose flour, and beaten egg whites into the yolk mixture until fully combined.
    • Divide the batter between the two pans and bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Cool completely.
  2. Prepare the Ricotta Cream:
    • Whip the heavy cream to soft peaks and set aside.
    • In a separate bowl, beat the ricotta, powdered sugar, and vanilla until smooth and creamy. Fold in the whipped cream gently to avoid deflating it.
  3. Cook the Pears:
    • In a small saucepan, combine the diced pears, sugar, lemon juice, and liqueur. Cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes until the pears soften slightly but still hold their shape. Cool completely.
  4. Assemble the Cake:
    • Place one sponge layer on a serving plate. Spread half of the ricotta cream evenly over the top, then layer with the cooled pears.
    • Add the second sponge layer and spread the remaining ricotta cream on top. Smooth the surface with a spatula.
  5. Chill and Garnish:
    • Refrigerate the cake for at least 4 hours (or overnight) to set. Before serving, dust with powdered sugar and garnish with toasted hazelnuts or chocolate shavings if desired.

Tips for Making Ricotta and Pear Cake

  1. Use Fresh Ricotta:
    • High-quality ricotta cheese ensures a creamy, smooth filling. Drain it well to avoid excess moisture.
  2. Choose Ripe Pears:
    • Use ripe but firm pears like Bartlett or Anjou for the best flavor and texture.
  3. Cook the Pears Lightly:
    • Cooking the pears with sugar and lemon juice enhances their sweetness and prevents them from browning.
  4. Cool the Layers:
    • Ensure the sponge layers and pear filling are completely cooled before assembling the cake to avoid melting the ricotta cream.
  5. Refrigerate Overnight:
    • Letting the cake rest overnight enhances the flavors and makes slicing easier.
  6. Hazelnut Flour Substitute:
    • If hazelnut flour is unavailable, grind whole hazelnuts finely in a food processor.

Best Time to Bake Ricotta and Pear Cake

Ricotta and Pear Cake is perfect for autumn and winter, as pears are in season and the cake’s creamy, nutty flavors complement colder weather. It’s also ideal for festive occasions like Christmas or as a light, refreshing dessert in early spring. However, thanks to its simple ingredients and versatility, it can be enjoyed year-round as an elegant dessert for dinner parties or family gatherings.
